Understanding Gender Equality

Gender equality means everyone — regardless of gender identity or expression — has equal rights, responsibilities, and opportunities. It isn't just about women; it benefits people of all genders, families, communities, and societies.

Learn why it matters

Why gender equality matters

Achieving gender equality involves removing barriers that prevent people from accessing opportunities, protecting people from gender-based violence, and changing social norms and institutions that reproduce inequality. It also means recognizing and valuing the contributions of everyone.

Human rights

Every person has the right to live free from discrimination and violence.

Economic benefits

Equal access to education and work increases productivity and strengthens economies.

Better societies

Inclusive decision-making produces fairer laws and stronger communities.

How you can help

  • Listen to and center the experiences of people from marginalized genders.
  • Call out unfair treatment and bias when you see it.
  • Support policies that promote equal opportunities in education, health, and work.
  • Learn — read resources, join community discussions, and keep an open mind.
